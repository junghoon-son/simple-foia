Use FOIA to create records that matter

Generate FOIA requests and official complaints. Every submission creates a federal record that can't be easily deleted.

What is FOIA?

FOIA (Freedom of Information Act) is a law that says the government has to show you its records if you ask.

Think of it like this: the government works for us, and we have a right to see what they're doing. When you send a FOIA request, you're saying "show me the documents about X."

Why it matters: Your request itself becomes a federal record. Even if they don't give you everything, they have to log that you asked. That paper trail can't be easily deleted — and it shows someone was paying attention.
